Of which: bunker price premium embedded in fixed cost

This is the basis for the "Bunker→orig" column above. Taking the projection's own bunker cost (which already carries the 3+9 rates), it backs out the implied consumption and re-prices it at the original budget rates — HSFO $780→$460, VLSFO/LSFO $850→$540, MGO $1,500→$760 per MT. The gap is the price premium sitting inside today's fixed cost/TEU. Per-TEU on projection volume. Reading it. Of the projection's $175.3M bunker bill, about $66.9M (~38%) is the higher fuel-price assumption rather than more sailing — roughly $83 per TEU network-wide. If bunker were costed at the original budget rates, fixed cost/TEU would fall most on the heavy fuel users: Red Sea $1,260→$1,090 ($170/TEU), AEM $1,142→$1,004 ($137), West Asia $664→$577 ($87) and the small new SH3 $3,083→$2,330 ($753, on tiny volume). In other words, a material slice of the 3+9's higher fixed cost/TEU is fuel price, not operational — useful to separate when judging cost performance.

Key Assumptions & Caveats Behind the Projection

  • Network redesign drives much of the gain. Large positive variances come from lanes not in the initial budget (CGX, SIS, HLX, RES, RTS, GTS), while budgeted lane RCS (−US$13.0M) is dropped — partly new-service launches vs discontinued ones, not pure same-store improvement.
  • CGX +US$58.2M assumes ISP CN–PKG revenue at US$300/TEU on ~99k TEU — the biggest swing and highly assumption-sensitive; per-box GP is thin (~US$585).
  • REX / Red Sea +US$50.6M: Red Sea OFT rate is missing; currently estimated on Trade's weighted-average rate (≈US$8.5M impact). Trade input still required.
  • AEKLF import land cost assumed at US$1,000/TEU (≈US$28M impact).
  • HLX ISP TH–PKG assumed US$200/TEU, PKG–TH US$50/TEU; AEM ECCL container cost assumed equal to COC (≈US$1.5M).
  • SGX RACINE 2616WE excluded (missing volume/OFT); estimated impact ≈US$3.4M. SGX vessel cost US$3.2M; ISP revenue booked under SGX, cost under REX.
